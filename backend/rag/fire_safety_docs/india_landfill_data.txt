MAJOR LANDFILL SITES IN INDIA - DATA AND GPS COORDINATES

1. GHAZIPUR LANDFILL, DELHI
- GPS: 28.6218°N, 77.3266°E
- Area: ~70 acres
- Height: ~65 meters (higher than Taj Mahal)
- Daily waste intake: ~2,500 tonnes
- Operational since: 1984 (exceeded capacity in 2002)
- Major fire incidents: 2017 (burned for days), 2020, 2022
- Nearby population: >1 million within 5km radius
- Risk factors: Extreme height, poor gas management, dense surrounding population

2. BHALSWA LANDFILL, DELHI
- GPS: 28.7357°N, 77.1635°E
- Area: ~40 acres
- Height: ~50 meters
- Daily waste intake: ~2,200 tonnes
- Operational since: 1993 (exceeded capacity in 2006)
- Major fire incidents: 2022 (April, multiple fires during heatwave)
- Nearby population: ~500,000 within 3km radius

3. OKHLA LANDFILL, DELHI
- GPS: 28.5303°N, 77.2787°E
- Area: ~50 acres
- Operational since: 1994
- Note: Partially decommissioned, waste-to-energy plant operational nearby

4. DEONAR LANDFILL, MUMBAI
- GPS: 19.0596°N, 72.9217°E
- Area: ~326 acres (one of the largest in Asia)
- Daily waste intake: ~5,500 tonnes
- Operational since: 1927 (nearly 100 years old)
- Major fire incidents: 2016 (massive fire, smoke visible from space, flights affected)
- Nearby population: >600,000 within 5km radius

5. PIRANA LANDFILL, AHMEDABAD
- GPS: 22.9808°N, 72.5614°E

6. MAVALLIPURA LANDFILL, BENGALURU

7. KODUNGAIYUR LANDFILL, CHENNAI
- GPS: 13.1412°N, 80.2649°E

NATIONAL STATISTICS:
- India generates approximately 62 million tonnes of solid waste per year.
- Only 20% of this waste is processed/treated.
- Over 3,159 dumpsites exist across India (as of CPCB 2021 report).
- Landfill fires account for significant PM2.5 and methane emissions contributing to air quality crises.
- The informal waste picking sector employs an estimated 1.5-4 million workers across India.
